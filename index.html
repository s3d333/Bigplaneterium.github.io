How big is it?

The Observable
Universe

The observable universe stretches about 93 billion light-years across. Beyond that we simply cannot see — light from those regions hasn't reached us yet.

93

How many galaxies?

Two Trillion
Galaxies

The Milky Way is one of an estimated 2 trillion galaxies. Each galaxy contains billions to trillions of stars — more than all the grains of sand on every beach on Earth.

2T

How old?

13.8 Billion
Years Old

The universe began with the Big Bang about 13.8 billion years ago. Our solar system didn't form until the universe was already 9 billion years old.

13.8

What's it made of?

Only 5% Is
Ordinary Matter

Only about 5% of the universe is made of ordinary matter. The rest is dark matter (27%) and dark energy (68%) — invisible forces we know exist but cannot directly observe.

5%

History

A history of space exploration

Key moments that changed our understanding of the universe. Drag the timeline sideways.

1957

Sputnik 1

The Soviet Union launches the world's first artificial satellite, kicking off the Space Race.

1961

First Human in Space

Yuri Gagarin completes one orbit of Earth in 108 minutes aboard Vostok 1.

1969

Moon Landing

Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin become the first humans to walk on the Moon. 600 million people watched live.

1977

Voyager Probes

NASA launches Voyager 1 and 2. They fly past all four outer planets before leaving the solar system.

1990

Hubble Launched

The Hubble Space Telescope enters orbit, giving humanity its clearest view of deep space.

2004

Spirit & Opportunity

NASA's twin rovers land on Mars and find clear evidence liquid water once flowed on its surface.

2021

James Webb Telescope

The most powerful telescope ever built launches on Christmas Day. Its first images show galaxies 13 billion years old.
