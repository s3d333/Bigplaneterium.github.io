Space Missions

From the first satellite to rovers on Mars — humanity has been pushing the boundaries of space exploration for over 60 years.

Greatest Missions of All Time

Eight missions that changed everything we know about space.

1957
🇷🇺

Sputnik 1

Soviet Union

The world's first artificial satellite. Sputnik 1 was the size of a beach ball and took just 98 minutes to orbit Earth. Its launch started the Space Race between the US and USSR.

1969
🇺🇸

Apollo 11

NASA

The mission that landed the first humans on the Moon. Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin walked on the lunar surface on July 20, 1969, while Michael Collins orbited above.

1977
🇺🇸

Voyager 1

NASA

Launched in 1977, Voyager 1 is the furthest human-made object from Earth. It has left our solar system entirely and is travelling through interstellar space at over 60,000 km/h.

1990
🇺🇸

Hubble Space Telescope

NASA / ESA

Hubble orbits Earth at 547 km above the surface and has taken over 1.5 million observations. It helped scientists calculate the age of the universe and discover its expansion is accelerating.

1997
🇺🇸

Mars Pathfinder

NASA

The first mission to deploy a rover on another planet. The Sojourner rover explored Mars for 83 days and sent back over 550 images, proving rovers could work on other planets.

1998
🌍

International Space Station

NASA / ESA / Roscosmos / JAXA / CSA

The largest structure ever assembled in space. The ISS has been continuously inhabited since November 2000 and orbits Earth 16 times a day at around 28,000 km/h.

2012
🇺🇸

Curiosity Rover

NASA

Curiosity landed on Mars using a never-before-seen sky crane system. It confirmed that Mars once had conditions suitable for microbial life and is still exploring today.

2021
🌍

James Webb Telescope

NASA / ESA / CSA

The most powerful space telescope ever built. Webb can see galaxies from over 13 billion years ago and has transformed our understanding of the early universe and exoplanets.

What's Coming Next?

The next chapter of space exploration is already being written.

Artemis Programme

NASA's Artemis missions aim to return humans to the Moon and establish a long-term lunar presence — including the first woman and first person of colour to walk on the Moon.

Mars Sample Return

A joint NASA and ESA mission to bring Martian rock samples back to Earth for the first time. Scientists hope the samples may contain evidence of ancient microbial life.

Europa Clipper

Launched in 2024, Europa Clipper will fly past Jupiter's moon Europa nearly 50 times to study its icy ocean — one of the most promising places to look for life in our solar system.

Crewed Mars Mission

Both NASA and SpaceX are working toward sending humans to Mars within the next decade. Such a mission would take around 7 months of travel each way.
